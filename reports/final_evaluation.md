## Evaluation Criteria

- Correctness · Helpfulness · Domain accuracy · Safety · Tone · Clarity ·
  Hallucination reduction · Professional response quality

## Stage-by-Stage Takeaways

1. **Base model:** Fluent but generic; not grounded in our domain facts; occasionally
   factually wrong relative to our policies (e.g. gift cards).
2. **SFT model:** Learns domain facts and the instruction/response format; answers
   become specific and mostly correct, but can still be inconsistent in tone or overly
   verbose, since SFT only ever sees one "correct" answer per question and never sees
   examples of what *not* to do.
3. **DPO model:** Keeps the SFT model's domain knowledge, but is explicitly optimized
   to prefer the *chosen* response style over the *rejected* style across 100 paired
   examples — i.e. correct, helpful, safe, professional, and domain-specific over wrong,
   incomplete, unsafe, rude, generic, or off-domain. This stage primarily improves
   **tone, confidence, and conciseness**, while SFT primarily provides the **factual/
   domain grounding**.

## Overall Conclusion

The final DPO-aligned model is the best-performing of the three for an e-commerce
support assistant: it retains the domain accuracy gained during instruction
fine-tuning while producing more consistently professional, confident, and
customer-friendly responses, directly reflecting the preferences encoded in
`data/preference_dataset.jsonl`.
